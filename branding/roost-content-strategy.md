## Comprehensive Keyword Research, Topic Clusters & Article Roadmap

## Executive Summary

This document maps the full content landscape for a self-service AI-powered cost segregation tool targeting STR/Airbnb investors with 1–5 properties ($300K–$2M). The research is grounded in competitor content analysis, search intent mapping, and the current regulatory environment (OBBBA signed July 4, 2025; 100% bonus depreciation permanently reinstated for property acquired after January 19, 2025).

The content strategy serves dual purposes:
1. **Lead generation** — driving traffic to the quiz funnel (free savings estimate)
2. **Authority building** — establishing Roost as the go-to educational resource for STR investors navigating cost segregation, depreciation, and passive activity rules

## Competitive Content Weaknesses to Exploit

1. **No competitor explains the catch-up study path clearly.** Most content assumes the reader just bought a property. Roost's target user bought 1–3 years ago and is just learning about cost seg.

2. **Furnishings treatment is universally glossed over.** The distinction between furnishings included in purchase price vs. post-purchase buildout is a key differentiator that no competitor addresses well.

3. **The OBBBA bifurcated 2025 rules are poorly explained.** Most content either ignores the January 19, 2025 cutoff or gets it wrong. Roost can be the definitive source.

4. **"What your CPA needs" content is almost nonexistent.** CPAs are the end-consumer of the cost seg report. Content explaining the deliverable requirements (PDF narrative + Excel fixed asset schedule) builds trust with both investors and their CPAs.

5. **No one explains the 27.5 vs. 39-year STR depreciation nuance well.** STRs that qualify as non-rental activities use 39-year schedules for the building, not 27.5 — and this is widely misunderstood.

## Key Regulatory Facts to Thread Through All Content

These facts should be consistently referenced across the content library to establish accuracy and authority:

1. **OBBBA (One Big Beautiful Bill Act)** — Signed July 4, 2025. Permanently reinstated 100% bonus depreciation for qualifying property acquired and placed in service after January 19, 2025.
2. **Bifurcated 2025 rates** — Property placed in service January 1–19, 2025 gets 40% bonus depreciation (TCJA phase-down). Property placed in service January 20+ gets 100% (OBBBA).
3. **Section 179 expansion** — OBBBA doubled the maximum deduction to $2.5M with a $4M phase-out threshold.
4. **QBI deduction permanent** — The 20% qualified business income deduction under Section 199A is now permanent (was set to expire end of 2025).
5. **Material participation** — 7 IRS tests; most STR owners use the 100-hour/more-than-anyone-else test or the 500-hour test.
6. **7-day rule** — Average guest stay of 7 days or fewer classifies STR as non-rental activity under Reg. §1.469-1T(e)(3)(ii)(A).
7. **Form 3115** — Automatic consent change (no user fee) under Rev. Proc. 2015-13; Section 481(a) catch-up adjustment claimed in current year.
8. **STR depreciation period** — 39 years for building (non-residential real property classification when treated as non-rental), not 27.5 years. This is widely misreported.
9. **State conformity varies** — Many states do not conform to federal bonus depreciation; content should flag this consistently.
10. **1099-K threshold** — For 2026, Airbnb required to issue 1099-K if gross payments exceed $600.
