# Abode — Brand Positioning

**Last updated:** 2026-03-16
**Framework:** StoryBrand

---

## The Core Narrative

Cost segregation has been in the tax code since 1997. Institutional real estate investors and private equity firms have used it to shelter millions in taxes for three decades. The only reason it wasn't on your return is that no one built it for you.

The IRS is holding your money. Abode helps you take it back.

---

## StoryBrand Map

### The Hero
STR owners who think like operators. Tech-forward. Hustle-native. They found the market, ran the numbers, listed the property, and optimized their Airbnb before most people knew it was a business model. They treat their STR as an investment, not a side project. Identity: **the side hustle class** — not landlords, not passive investors. Builders.

**Their desire:** Make the investment work as hard as they do. Every dollar counts. They want to feel like they're playing the same game as the people above them.

### The Problem

- **External:** They've been overpaying taxes every year — often $40K–$150K+ over the life of the property — without knowing it.
- **Internal:** They did everything right. Took the risk, found the deal, ran the numbers. And somehow the real money is still going somewhere else.
- **Philosophical:** This strategy is legal, proven, and has existed for 30 years. The only reason everyday STR owners don't use it is that no one had an incentive to make it accessible. That's not a knowledge gap — that's gatekeeping.

### The Villain
**Opacity.** Not the CPA, not the IRS — the system. Cost segregation was buried in a 70,000-page tax code, priced behind $5K–$15K engineering studies, and designed for institutional players. No conspiracy — just a tool that was never built for the individual investor.

> CPAs are well-meaning allies, not adversaries. They didn't bring it up because the economics didn't work for residential STR owners before. That's changed.

### The Guide (Abode)
- **Empathy:** You've already done the hard part. You shouldn't have to fight the tax code too.
- **Authority:** Cost segregation is a proven, IRS-recognized methodology. We've digitized a process that used to cost $5,000–$15,000 and take weeks. We follow the IRS Cost Segregation Audit Techniques Guide. We deliver in minutes.

### The Plan
1. Enter your property — address, purchase price, a few details. 60 seconds.
2. Get your analysis — estimated savings, broken down by asset class.
3. Claim what's yours — hand the study to your CPA. They file it. Done.

### Call to Action
**Primary:** "See what you're owed"
**Secondary:** "Calculate my savings — free"

Both frames lean into *angle 3*: the money already belongs to you. Abode shows you where it is.

### Success
They walk into tax season knowing exactly what they're getting back. They reinvest it in the next property. They're not just an STR owner anymore — they're building real wealth the same way institutional players always have. They're finally in the game.

### Failure (cost of inaction)
Every year without a study is a year the IRS keeps their money. Concretely: a renovation, a down payment, a year of mortgage payments — handed over because they didn't know to ask.

> "Every year you wait is a year the IRS keeps your money."

## Positioning Angles (Approved)

### Angle 1 — The Insider Strategy
Something the wealthy have always known but kept quiet. Framing: exclusivity → injustice → democratization.
> "A 30-year tax strategy that was never built for you. Until now."

### Angle 3 — It's Your Money
The IRS is holding money that belongs to you. Not a loophole — a reclassification. It reduces risk perception and reframes asking as reclaiming.
> "The IRS is holding your money. Time to claim it back."

### Identity Frame — Side Hustle Class
Not landlords. Not passive investors. Active operators who found the market early, run their STR like a business, and want institutional-grade tools. Tech-forward. Hustle-native.

---

## Angles to Avoid

- **CPA blind spot framing** — Don't position the CPA as missing something or failing the client. CPAs are gatekeepers and referral sources. Keep them as well-meaning allies limited by old economics, not negligent advisors.
- **"Landlord class"** — Feels passive, dated, and carries negative social connotations. Use: STR investor, operator, builder.
